# Agentic AI for Ross Stores — Tariff-Aware Procurement Advisor

## 1. Company & value-chain placement

**Ross Stores, Inc.** ("Ross Dress for Less") is a US off-price apparel and
home-fashion retailer (~2,200 stores, ~$21B revenue). Its entire business model
rests on **opportunistic buying**: merchants procure branded goods below
wholesale and resell 20–60% under department-store prices.

**Value-chain function chosen: Sourcing & Procurement (Operations / Buying).**
This is Ross's most decision-dense function — hundreds of buyers make thousands
of buy/no-buy calls per season across brands and origin countries.

## 2. Problem statement

> **"When, and in what quantity, should Ross procure a given product from a
> given brand and origin country so that the buy remains profitably feasible —
> given current tariffs, the state of geopolitical trade deals, and consumer
> demand trends? And when the answer is 'partially yes', exactly how many units
> should we commit to?"**

Concretely, for every candidate buy (brand × origin × category × quantity), the
buyer must answer:

1. **Feasibility** — after import duty, freight and handling, does the landed
   cost still clear the target gross margin at Ross's off-price retail point?
2. **Risk** — is the origin country's trade relationship with the US stable
   (active trade deal), deteriorating (tariff escalation, export controls), or
   improving (relief being ratified)?
3. **Demand** — is the category trending up or down with consumers, and what
   does next quarter's demand forecast support?
4. **Quantity & timing** — full commitment, a **partial buy** (minimum order
   quantity but below the requested volume, capping exposure while the
   situation clarifies), or walk away — and should the order be placed now or
   after a known upcoming event (e.g., a ratification vote)?

## 3. Why this needs *agentic* AI (not a dashboard, not a single prompt)

| Requirement of the problem | Why agents |
|---|---|
| Four **heterogeneous evidence streams** (tariff schedules, trade-deal news, demand indices, unit economics) each need different data and different reasoning styles | Each stream gets a **specialist agent** with its own data slice, mandate and output schema — mirroring how a real buying office splits the work |
| The final call requires **synthesis and judgment**, including graded outcomes ("partial yes, 8,000 units, wait for the October vote") | A **Decision Agent** consumes the four specialist reports and produces a structured, auditable recommendation — something a static rule engine cannot do for novel geopolitical situations |
| Conditions change weekly (new tariff tranches, port congestion, deal ratifications) | Agents **re-run on fresh data** without re-engineering; the reasoning adapts to events it has never seen |
| Buyers must be able to **audit** the recommendation | Hard numbers (landed cost, margin, forecast) are computed deterministically in code; the LLM contributes interpretation and the final structured decision — every agent's report is shown to the user |

A traditional BI dashboard can *show* the tariff rate; it cannot *argue* that a
Vietnam buy should be split into a partial order now and the balance after the
October tariff-relief ratification vote. That judgment-with-evidence step is
exactly what an agentic pipeline supplies.

## 4. The agent team

| Agent | Mandate | Data slice |
|---|---|---|
| **Tariff Agent** | Duty exposure, landed-cost impact, cheaper-origin alternatives | `tariff_schedule.csv` + landed-cost math |
| **Geopolitics Agent** | Trade-deal status, country risk score, 6-month outlook | `trade_agreements.csv`, `geopolitical_events.csv` |
| **Trend Agent** | Demand momentum, seasonality, next-quarter forecast | `market_trends.csv`, `sales_history.csv` |
| **Finance Agent** | Landed cost vs. target margin, breakeven retail, negotiation levers | brand catalog + tariff output |
| **Chief Decision Agent** | Synthesizes all four reports → **YES / PARTIAL_YES / NO**, recommended quantity (guard-railed to MOQ and request), order timing, conditions to revisit | All agent outputs |

Orchestration: sequential pipeline in `agents.py` (`run_pipeline`), LLM = Google
Gemini with JSON-schema-constrained outputs; deterministic guardrails clamp the
recommended quantity to `[MOQ, requested]` in multiples of 500 and force 0 on NO.

## 5. Data (synthetic, mid-2026 calibrated)

Six datasets generated by `data_gen.py` (seeded, reproducible): 24 candidate
brands across 8 origin countries and 5 categories; base-vs-current tariff
schedule reflecting 2025–26 tariff actions; per-country trade-agreement status;
a 12-item geopolitical event feed with risk scores; 24 months of demand/social
buzz indices; 24 months of Ross category sales.

## 6. Success criteria

- Every recommendation states **decision, quantity, timing, confidence,
  rationale, and revisit-triggers**.
- Margin math is exact and reproducible; the same inputs give the same landed
  cost.
- A buyer can flip origin country (e.g., China → Vietnam → Mexico) and watch
  the recommendation change coherently with tariffs and risk.
